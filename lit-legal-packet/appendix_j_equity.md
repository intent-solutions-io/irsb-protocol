| Term | Value |

| **Vesting Schedule** | Monthly after cliff |
| **Exercise Price** | Fair Market Value (FMV) at grant date |
| **Issuing Entity** | [Client legal entity to be confirmed] |
| **Option Type** | Non-Qualified Stock Option (NSO) |

### Key Terms Explained

**Fully Diluted (FD):** Percentage calculated on all outstanding shares plus all shares reserved for issuance under equity plans, options, warrants, and convertible securities.

**Cliff:** Provider must remain engaged for minimum cliff period before any options vest.

**Monthly Vesting:** After cliff, options vest in equal monthly installments over remaining vesting period.

**Exercise Price:** Price at which options may be exercised, set at FMV on grant date per IRS requirements.

## J.4 FALLBACK MECHANISM

If equity grant documentation is not executed by Day 30:

### Client's Election
Client must elect within **10 business days** (by Day 45):

**Option A: Execute Equity Documentation**
- Client executes equity documentation promptly (within 15 additional days)
- Vesting begins from original Agreement date (retroactive)
- No cash conversion

**Option B: Cash Conversion**
- Parties negotiate in good faith a cash equivalent
- Cash amount to be mutually agreed
- Paid within 30 days of agreement on amount

### Cash Conversion Principles
If converting to cash:
- Base calculation: Number of shares × most recent 409A valuation per share
- Negotiation range: 80-120% of base calculation
- Payment terms: Single lump sum or installments by agreement

### If No Agreement Reached
If parties cannot agree on cash conversion terms within 30 days:
- Matter submitted to mediation per Section 14 of Master Agreement
- Mediator determines fair cash equivalent
- Decision binding on both parties

## J.5 ACCELERATION

### Single Trigger Acceleration

Upon a **Change of Control** event:
- **25%** of then-unvested options shall immediately vest
- Remaining unvested options continue normal vesting schedule
- Vested options remain exercisable per option agreement terms

### Change of Control Definition
"Change of Control" means:
- Sale of substantially all assets
- Merger or consolidation where existing stockholders own less than 50%
- Acquisition of more than 50% of voting securities
- Replacement of majority of board in 12-month period

### No Double Trigger
This Agreement provides single-trigger acceleration only. No additional acceleration is provided upon involuntary termination following Change of Control.

## J.6 MOST FAVORED NATION (MFN) CLAUSE

### MFN Protection

If Client grants more favorable advisory equity terms to a similarly situated advisor or contractor within **90 days** of Agreement execution, Client will:
- Notify Provider within 10 business days
- Offer Provider equivalent terms upon request

### "Similarly Situated" Definition
An advisor or contractor is similarly situated if:
- Providing services of comparable scope and value
- In a similar engagement structure (fixed-price, time-limited)
- Not a full-time employee or executive

### MFN Exclusions
MFN does not apply to:
- Employee grants under standard compensation plans
- Founder equity
- Investor-related grants
- Grants in connection with acquisitions

## J.7 SEPARATION FROM DELIVERABLES

### Independence of Obligations

> **SEPARATION CLAUSE:** Equity incentives are provided for alignment purposes and are **separate from, and do not modify**:
>
> - The Deliverables defined in Appendix A
> - The Acceptance Criteria for any Deliverable
> - Payment obligations for cash compensation
> - Provider's obligation to perform the Services

### Non-Issuance Effects

If equity is not issued:
- Provider remains obligated to deliver all Deliverables
- Client remains obligated to pay all cash Fees
- Neither party may withhold performance based on equity status
- Fallback mechanism in Section J.4 provides sole remedy

### No Leverage

Neither party shall:
- Condition Deliverable acceptance on equity terms
- Withhold payment pending equity resolution
- Delay Services pending equity documentation

## J.8 SUSPENSION RIGHTS (LIMITED)

### General Prohibition
Provider shall not suspend Services based on equity status alone.

Provider may suspend **non-essential administrative tasks** (not Deliverables) if:

**Non-essential work does NOT include:**
